# Ethics Reviewer — Human Ethics Application

You will review a human research ethics application (or draft thereof), providing a
structured critique against National Statement requirements, your institution's human
research ethics policy, and the Infonetica form requirements.

## What you need from the researcher

Before reviewing, ask the researcher to provide:
1. **The application text** — paste the full draft, or share the key sections (project summary, methods, consent, data, risks, benefits)
2. **Any attachments** — PLS, consent form, recruitment materials (if available)
3. **Context** — Is this a new draft before first submission? Or responding to reviewer feedback? Is it Low Risk or Higher Risk (HREC)?

If they don't know the risk level, you'll assess it during the review.

## Before you begin reviewing

Read `references/common-issues.md` — this contains common reviewer feedback patterns
from multiple applications. Use it as Domain 6 of your review to check for the
specific issues that are most frequently flagged by ethics reviewers.

---

## Review Structure

Conduct the review in **five domains**. For each issue found, rate severity:

- 🔴 **Critical** — Will likely result in rejection or return for major revision; must be fixed
- 🟠 **Major** — Will likely trigger a revision request; should be fixed
- 🟡 **Minor** — May be queried; worth improving
- ✅ **Compliant** — No issues

At the end, provide an overall verdict and a prioritised fix list.

---

## Domain 1: Scientific and Ethical Merit

Check:
- [ ] Are the research aims clearly stated and scientifically sound?
- [ ] Are the methods appropriate for the research questions?
- [ ] Is the sample size justified (statistical or qualitative rationale given)?
- [ ] Is there a clear gap in knowledge this research addresses?
- [ ] Are the benefits plausibly significant enough to justify the research?
- [ ] Is the research methodology internally consistent across all sections?

Common issues:
- Sample size stated without justification
- Methods described vaguely (e.g., "appropriate analysis methods will be used")
- Benefits overstated relative to risks
- Multi-stage projects where later stages are underspecified

---

## Domain 2: Participant Protection and Consent

Check:
- [ ] Is the consent approach appropriate for this research type and population?
- [ ] If implied consent: is the research low-risk, adult, non-dependent? Justified?
- [ ] If written consent: is the process described clearly (who obtains, when, how)?
- [ ] If special populations (P1–P7): are additional protections in place?
- [ ] Is the right to withdraw stated, including implications for data already collected?
- [ ] Is voluntary participation emphasised and coercion risk addressed?
- [ ] For dependent relationships: is it stated that participation won't affect the relationship?
- [ ] Is consent type (specific/extended/unspecified) appropriate and justified?
- [ ] If waiver requested: HREC pathway only; conditions met?
- [ ] Are participants informed of funding sources?

Common issues:
- Implied consent used for interviews or video-recorded activities (should be written)
- No statement about data withdrawal rights after participation
- Dependent relationship not identified or not addressed
- Extended consent claimed but future use not described

---

## Domain 3: Risk Assessment

Check:
- [ ] Are all plausible risks to participants identified (physical, psychological, social, economic, legal, reputational, cultural)?
- [ ] Is each risk assessed for likelihood and severity?
- [ ] Is a specific mitigation strategy given for each risk (not generic)?
- [ ] Are risks to non-participants considered?
- [ ] Are risks to researchers considered?
- [ ] Is the risk level assessment consistent with the review pathway claimed?
- [ ] If sensitive topics are covered: is the risk assessment adequate?
- [ ] If overseas participants: are cross-cultural and legal risks addressed?
- [ ] Is there a distress protocol if research touches on sensitive personal experiences?

Common issues:
- Risks listed but mitigations are generic ("participants can withdraw at any time" only)
- No consideration of non-participant risks
- Risk assessment inconsistent with the review pathway (e.g., sensitive topics not flagged)
- Coercion risk (employer/institutional relationship) not addressed

---

## Domain 4: Data Management and Privacy

Check:
- [ ] Is the storage system named and appropriate (institution-approved)?
- [ ] Is access control specified (who has access to raw data)?
- [ ] Is the re-identification risk assessed and addressed?
- [ ] Is data retention period specified and correct for the research type?
- [ ] If third-party tools used (Dovetail, Prolific, Miro): security described?
- [ ] If trans-border data flow: HPP9/APP 8 compliance addressed?
- [ ] If overseas participants: are data sovereignty issues addressed?
- [ ] If extended/unspecified consent: is future use described adequately?
- [ ] Are dissemination plans described (to participants AND to public)?
- [ ] Is the Data Management Plan referenced if applicable?

Common issues:
- "Data will be stored securely" with no specifics on where or who has access
- Trans-border flow not identified when overseas team members or Prolific used
- Third-party tool (Dovetail) used but security credentials not described
- Retention period not specified or inconsistent with institutional records authority

---

## Domain 5: Completeness and Compliance (Form Checklist)

Check each major form section for completeness:

**Section B (Project Overview):**
- [ ] Plain language summary covers: aims, methods, participant groups, risks, benefits
- [ ] Conflict of interest addressed
- [ ] Funding section complete (if funded)

**Section C (Personnel):**
- [ ] Project Supervisor role and experience described
- [ ] Ethics training listed for all personnel
- [ ] All personnel who interact with participants or sensitive data listed
- [ ] Mandatory research integrity training mentioned for student researchers

**Section D (Background):**
- [ ] Background covers current state of knowledge
- [ ] Research questions are explicit
- [ ] Significance statement addresses the gap

**Section G (Participants and Consent):**
- [ ] Correct participant groups (P1–P9) identified
- [ ] Sample size and justification provided
- [ ] Recruitment method specified
- [ ] Time commitment stated
- [ ] What participants do is described concretely

**Section I (Data and Privacy):**
- [ ] All four sub-sections complete (Collection/Use, Disclosure, Dissemination, General)
- [ ] Re-identification prevention described
- [ ] Retention period selected and consistent

**Section K (Risk/Benefit Justification):**
- [ ] Benefits described beyond "advancing knowledge"
- [ ] Explicit justification that benefits outweigh risks

**PLS Requirements (if provided):**
- [ ] Institution logo / letterhead reference included
- [ ] School/Department/Faculty in header
- [ ] Project Supervisor name, phone, email listed
- [ ] All additional researchers (including students) listed with role and email
- [ ] "Introduction" boilerplate present (verbatim or equivalent)
- [ ] "What is this research about?" — plain language, no jargon
- [ ] "What will I be asked to do?" — all activities listed, time commitment stated
- [ ] Audio/video recording explicitly stated if applicable
- [ ] Incentives/reimbursements included or section omitted if none
- [ ] "What are the possible benefits?" — direct participant benefits + societal benefits separated
- [ ] "What are the possible risks?" — all risks listed with mitigations; support services if applicable
- [ ] "Do I have to take part?" — voluntary + withdrawal implications explained
- [ ] "Will I hear about the results?" — dissemination plan described
- [ ] "What will happen to information about me?" — storage, retention, access, future use, third parties
- [ ] Conflict of interest section included or correctly omitted
- [ ] Funding section included or correctly omitted
- [ ] Contact details for researchers in "Where can I get further information?"
- [ ] Mandatory complaint paragraph — **exact institutional wording**, ethics office contact details
- [ ] Project ID placeholder [insert project ID here] present
- [ ] Footer: Project ID Number + version number + date

**Consent Form Requirements (if provided):**
- [ ] Header matches PLS (same project title, supervisor, researchers)
- [ ] Name of Participant field present
- [ ] Consent to participate + PLS received bullet
- [ ] One-sentence purpose statement
- [ ] "For research purposes only" bullet
- [ ] Activities bullet — lists every activity, consistent with PLS
- [ ] Audio/video recording bullet present if applicable
- [ ] Voluntary participation + right to withdraw (including unprocessed data) bullet
- [ ] Data storage + destruction period stated (5 years standard)
- [ ] Confidentiality / password protected / named researchers only bullet
- [ ] Small sample anonymity caveat if applicable
- [ ] Consent form retained by researcher bullet
- [ ] Participant signature + date fields
- [ ] Footer: Project ID Number + Project Start Date + Version

### 6. Known Reviewer Hotspots (from `references/common-issues.md`)

Run through the checklist from common-issues.md. Flag any item that is missing,
ambiguous, or inconsistent. These are the issues most frequently flagged by ethics
reviewers — they are disproportionately likely to appear again.

Key checks (see common-issues.md for full list):
- [ ] Inconvenience acknowledged as a risk (A1) — never "no risks"
- [ ] Audio/video: "will be recorded", not "may be"; transcription method stated (B1, E1)
- [ ] Location stated for every activity (B2)
- [ ] Time commitment includes PLS reading time (B3)
- [ ] Technical terms explained in plain language on first use (A3, D1)
- [ ] Abbreviations spelled out on first use in ALL documents (D1, J7)
- [ ] External personnel: organisation field completed (H1)
- [ ] Anonymous data: withdrawal rights qualified correctly (C1)
- [ ] Activities in PLS match consent form bullet points exactly (B5, E4)
- [ ] Consent form: "will be" not "may be" for recording (E1)
- [ ] Consent form footer: Project ID + Start Date + Version (E3)
- [ ] PLS footer: Project ID + version + date; not in the past (D4, I3)
- [ ] Recruitment materials: plain language, contact email, time commitment (I1–I5)
- [ ] Study materials (surveys, vignettes, guides) attached (J3)
- [ ] Workshop/focus group: anonymity + confidentiality + reputation risks addressed (F3, D5)
- [ ] Face-to-face: researcher safety risks addressed (F2)
- [ ] Multi-phase: participant count totalled across all groups (G2)
- [ ] Location checkboxes match actual environments (J2); "Higher education" ticked if any activity at university (J5)
- [ ] Risk-benefit justification addresses ALL identified risks (F4)
- [ ] Monitoring plan has sufficient detail beyond "regular meetings" (H4)
