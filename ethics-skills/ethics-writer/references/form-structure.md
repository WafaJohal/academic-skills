# Infonetica ERM Form Structure — Human Ethics Application

Complete map of all sections and questions in the Infonetica form, in order.
Use this to ensure every section of the drafted application is complete.

---

## SECTION A: Application Details

- Is this an amendment? (Yes / No — new application)
- Is this application linked to another ethics approval? (Yes / No)
- Application Type: Project Application / Student Coursework Program / Transfer Application
- Project title

### Project type (check all that apply):
- Staff Research Project
- Practical Class (student learning skills)
- Funded Consultancy
- Supervised Student Research Project → sub-select: PhD / Masters by Research / Masters by Coursework / Honours / Postgraduate Diploma / Advanced Medical Science / Doctorate / Undergraduate with Research component / Other
- Other → specify

- When do you propose to commence? (As soon as approved / Specific start date)
- Proposed date of project completion

---

## SECTION B: Project Overview

### Summarise your research project in plain language
Cover: broad aims / methods / participant groups (who, what they do, how consent given) / risks / benefits

### Conflict of Interest
- Does your research involve any conflict of interest — potential, real or perceived? (Yes / No)
- If yes: describe

### Funded Projects
- Is this a funded research project? (Yes / No)
- If yes: Internal / External-Funding Agency / External-Contract Research
- Funder name (from Themis list or text)
- Funding amount ($AUD)
- Themis agreement ID
- Will participants be informed of the source of funding? (Yes / No) → explain how

---

## SECTION C: Project Supervisor

For the Project Supervisor:
- Title / First Name / Surname
- Department / Faculty
- Is this project conducted under a different department? (Yes / No)
- Honorary staff member? (Yes / No)
- Phone / Email
- Personnel type (Staff)
- Summary of this person's role in the project
- Experience, qualifications and competence relevant to their role
- Additional training planned? (text or N/A)
- Ethics training and clearances (check all):
  - Experience as a Human Ethics Committee Member
  - Experience as a Research Ethics Advisor
  - Familiar with the National Statement
  - Familiar with Indigenous guidelines
  - Working with children check
  - Safe Radiation Practices – Ionising
  - Safe Radiation Practices – Use Licence Holders
  - Good Clinical Practice (GCP) training
  - Other → specify

### Other Internal Personnel (repeat block for each person)
- Are there other internal personnel? (Yes / No)
- Same fields as above plus: Personnel type (Staff/Student) / Project role (Investigator/Associated Personnel/etc.)

### External Personnel (repeat block for each person)
- Are there external personnel? (Yes / No)
- Title / First Name / Surname / Department / Organisation / Phone / Email
- Personnel type (External) / Project role
- Role summary / Experience / Additional training / Ethics training checklist

---

## SECTION D: Background

### Background
Free text: current state of research/knowledge, key references (a few, not full list).
Reference: NS Chapter 3.1 Elements, ss. 5.3.1 and 5.3.5.

### Key Question(s)
What are the key research questions the project intends to examine?

### Significance of this Research
How does this research fill a gap in existing knowledge? Why does it matter?

## SECTION E: Location

### Where will the research team be physically located? (check all)
- On site – [Your Institution]
- Off site – External Sites within your state/territory
- Off site – External Sites within your country
- Off site – External Sites within your country (outside your state/territory)
- Off site – Outside your country
- Not finalised at this stage/other

Clinic(s) / Community centre(s) / Cultural/religious organisation(s) / Educational settings / Higher education (University/TAFE) / Hospital(s) / Online / Private residence(s) / Professional organisation(s) / Public place(s) / Research institute(s) / Workplace(s) / Studio(s) / Other

- Human beings

- Existing biospecimens only
- Existing data only

### Health-related intervention? (Yes / No)

### Select all methods to be used (check all that apply):
Action research / Biospecimen analysis / Data linkage / Epidemiology / Ethnographic methods / Human Body Donor Research / Interventional/Clinical Trials / Ionising radiation / Observation / Surveys/interviews/focus groups / Textual analysis / Usability study / Other

For checked methods, describe if substantially varying from standard definition.

## SECTION G: Participants

### Specific participant groups targeted? (check all)
- [P1] Women who are pregnant or the human fetus
- [P2] Children and young people
- [P3] People in dependent or unequal relationships
- [P4] People highly dependent on medical care who may be unable to give consent
- [P5] People with a cognitive impairment, intellectual disability, or mental illness
- [P6] People who may be involved in illegal activities
- [P7] Aboriginal or Torres Strait Islander Peoples
- [P8] People in countries other than Australia
- [P9] Other participant group → specify

### Adults without decision-making capacity? (Yes / No)

### Consent and information — all participant groups

#### How will you obtain informed consent? (check all)
- Written consent will be sought
- Verbal consent will be sought
- Consent implied by action (e.g., ticking a box, proceeding to next screen)
- Third parties (parents/guardians/carers) will provide consent on behalf of participants
- Third parties (community elders, school boards) involved in whole-community decisions
- Opt-out approach
- Waiver of consent requested

#### Explain why you have chosen this form of consent (free text)

### For each participant group (P1–P9 as applicable):

#### Describe how recruitment will occur
Include: sample description, basis for sample choice, demographic characteristics, eligibility constraints, how participants identified and approached, who does this.

#### Estimated sample size (number)

#### Justification for sample size (free text, include statistical details if applicable)

#### What will participants be asked to do?
Include approximate time commitment.

#### Consent in different manner for this group? (Yes / No)

#### Data collection different for this group? (Yes / No)

### Potential Risks to Participants (per group)
- Potential risks to this participant group
- Risk management strategy

### Information for this participant group
- Will information be provided differently than described on general consent page? (Yes / No)

## SECTION H: Risks to Non-Participants and Researchers

### Non-participants
- Does research pose any potential risks to non-participants? (Yes / No)
- If yes: potential risks / risk management strategy
- If no: explain why no risk

### Researchers
- Does research pose any potential risks to researchers or independent contractors? (Yes / No)
- If yes: potential risks / risk management strategy
- If no: explain why no risk

  - EXTENDED (future related projects)
  - UNSPECIFIED (any future research)
  - NA – Waiver of consent

- Seeking approval for collection from a third party? (Yes / No)

## SECTION I: Data and Privacy — General Issues

- Project involve unique identifiers from other agencies? (Yes / No)
- What measures will be taken to prevent re-identification of participants?
- Which will research generate? Non-digital data / Digital data

### Security and Storage of Non-Digital Data (if applicable)
- How stored short-term and long-term? Whose responsibility?
- Who has access to unprocessed (raw) data? What security measures?

### Security and Storage of Digital Data
- How stored short-term and long-term? Whose responsibility?
- Who has access to unprocessed (raw) data? What security measures?

### Databanks
- Will data be stored in a databank for future research? (Yes / No)

### Trans-border data flow
- Does project involve trans-border data flow? (Yes / No)
- If yes: explain how carried out in accordance with HPP9, APP 8

### Data retention
- How long will data be kept? (check one)
  - Summary record (Permanent – University Archives)
  - Regulatory/community-wide significance (Permanent)
  - Clinical trials (Temporary – 15 years after completion)
  - Research involving minors (Temporary – 15 years after child turns 18)
  - All others (Temporary – 5 years after completion)
  - Perpetuity
  - Other
- Do you have a Data Management Plan? (Yes / No)

## SECTION J: Project Management and Monitoring

- How will researchers manage and monitor the research?
- Project management (who manages, how compliance ensured, how multiple researchers coordinated)
- Monitoring (supervision arrangements, especially for students/junior researchers/overseas)
- Will independent contractors be involved? (Yes / No)

## SECTION K: Risks, Benefits and Justification

### Describe the expected benefits of this research
Benefits to community/society; specific benefits to participants (beyond positive feelings of participation).

### Justification of risks relative to expected benefits
Explain how expected benefits justify risks. Pay particular attention to risks greater than inconvenience.
