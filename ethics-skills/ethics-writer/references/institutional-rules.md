> - Confirmation that the project has human research ethics approval, with the project ID

## Mandatory PLS Requirements Checklist

Every PLS must include:
- Institution logo prominently placed (or on institutional letterhead if printed)
- Department(s)/School(s)/Faculty(-ies) involved
- Project title
- Name(s) and contact details of Principal Researcher and Other Researchers
- For student projects: student's level of study
- Purpose of the research project
- What participants will be asked to do + estimated time commitment
- If audio/video recorded: explicitly state this
- Risks arising from participation + minimisation procedures
- Expected benefits (to wider community; specific to participants if applicable)
- Any payments, incentives, or reimbursements
- Statement that involvement is voluntary and participants may withdraw at any time
- Explanation of withdrawal implications (can they withdraw data?)
- Likelihood and form of dissemination of results
- Confidentiality arrangements; any legal limitations
- If small sample size: note that this may make participants identifiable
- Data retention period and whether data may be used in future research
- Project ethics ID, date, and version number (in footer if printed)
- The mandatory complaint paragraph (above)
- If externally funded: amount(s) and source(s)
- If conflicts of interest: disclose
- If dependent relationships: statement that participation decisions won't affect relationship

## Risk Pathway Determination

**Lower Risk (LR Delegate review):**
- No risk of harm or discomfort (potential minor burden/inconvenience only), OR
- Risk of discomfort but no risk of harm (and foreseeable burden)

**Higher Risk (HREC review):**
- Research where risk of harm or significant risk of harm to participants or non-participants

**Key risk indicators that push to HREC:**
- P1–P7 special participant groups (especially P2 children, P7 ATSI, P3 dependent relationships)
- Deception or limited disclosure
- Collection/use/disclosure of personal health information without consent
- Clinical trials
- Ionising radiation
- Sensitive topics (suicide, self-harm, eating disorders, trauma, etc.)
- Waiver of consent

## Approved Data Storage Systems

Reference your institution's approved storage systems when relevant — they demonstrate compliance.
Common examples used in Australian university HCI/IS research include:

- **Qualtrics** (institution instance) — recommended for online surveys; confirm hosting location
- **REDCap** — recommended for clinical/health data
- **Institution-approved cloud storage** (e.g., OneDrive, SharePoint) — access controlled to named researchers
- **Microsoft Teams** — for video interviews/recordings (confirm institutional licence)
- **Miro** — acceptable for participatory workshops (note: third-party tool, note security protocols)
- **Dovetail** — acceptable for qualitative analysis (Australian company, AWS-hosted, AES-256 encryption, TLS 1.3, compliant with Australian Privacy Act) — always download and delete from Dovetail post-analysis
- **Prolific** — acceptable recruitment platform; note anonymous link delivery to survey platform

**NOT acceptable without justification:** Personal Google Drive, personal Dropbox, personal email for data

Always verify which specific systems your institution has approved before listing them in an application.

## Data Retention Rules

| Data type | Retention period |
|-----------|-----------------|
| Most human research datasets | 5 years after completion of research activity |
| Clinical trials | 15 years after completion |
| Research involving minors | 15 years after the child reaches age 18 |
| Datasets of regulatory/community-wide significance | Permanent (University Archives) |
| Summary records of research data | Permanent (University Archives) |

Note: "completion" means end of the research activity, not end of data collection.
Confirm these periods against your institution's Records & Disposal Authority.

## Overseas Research — National Statement Chapter 4.8 Requirements

When research involves overseas participants (P8) or overseas locations:
- Research outside Australia requires Faculty approval
- Must comply with NS and local values; give no less protection than NS
- Identify if local ethics review processes exist
- Address GDPR if EU participants involved
- Address whether any sensitive cultural issues or government-criticism risks exist
- Confirm trans-border data flow arrangements (HPP9, APP 8 compliance)
- Confirm no identifiable/sensitive personal data crosses borders
- Use institution-approved storage systems (data stays on Australian servers where possible)

## Conflict of Interest — Key Considerations for Funded Research

Funded evaluations and consultancies face additional scrutiny. Address:
- Researcher autonomy vs funder control
- Conflicts of interest (researcher/consultant/funder obligations)
- Dependent relationships in recruitment (has funder pre-specified the sample?)
- Consent: can participants actually say no if employer is funding?
- Dissemination: can funder restrict publication? How will this be managed?
- Level of independence/collaboration (co-design with funder?)

Reference: NS Chapter 5.6 and your institution's conflicts of interest policy.

## Common Consent Approaches by Research Type

| Research type | Typical consent approach |
|--------------|------------------------|
| Online anonymous surveys (Prolific, mailing lists) | Implied — proceeding past information screen = consent |
| Interviews (audio/video recorded) | Written/signed (via DocuSign or similar) |
| Participatory workshops | Written/signed prior to session |
| Site visits / contextual inquiry | Written/signed on site |
| Delphi studies (expert panels) | Written/signed (email confirmation acceptable) |
| Secondary analysis of de-identified data | Often waiver or exemption |

## Participant Groups — Special Requirements

**P2 Children and young people:**
- Separate PLS for children (age-appropriate language) AND for parents/guardians
- Assent from child + parental consent (or child-only consent if appropriate per NS)

**P3 Dependent relationships:**
- Must explicitly state participation decisions will not affect the dependent relationship
- e.g., "Students' grades will not be affected if they decline to participate"

**P7 ATSI Peoples:**
- Must follow AIATSIS Code of Ethics for Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander Research 2020
- Consult community; specific cultural considerations required
- Requires HREC review

**P8 Overseas participants:**
- See Chapter 4.8 requirements above
- Faculty approval required for overseas research locations

## Mandatory Research Integrity Training

All student researchers conducting human research must complete their institution's mandatory
research integrity training before undertaking data collection.
This should be listed under Ethics training for student personnel.
Check your institution's current training requirements and module names.

## Ethics ID Naming Convention

Documents should be named: `id_[PROJECT_ID]_[document_type]_[participant_group]_v[version]`
e.g., `id_12345_PLS_All_v1.docx`, `id_12345_consent_form_delphi_v2.docx`

Version date and version number must appear on all participant-facing documents.
